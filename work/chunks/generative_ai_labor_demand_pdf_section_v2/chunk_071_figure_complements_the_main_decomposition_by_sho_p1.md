In the earlier part of the sample, the composition effect accounts for the largest share of aggregate generative AI exposure change, consistent with the interpretation that the initial increase in exposure is driven mainly by reallocation in hiring shares across job cells. In the later part of the sample, the relative contribution of the within-cell exposure effect becomes larger, indicating that changes within job cells play an increasingly important role in lowering aggregate exposure. The interaction term remains smaller than the other two components in most quarters, although its contribution rises around some turning points.
